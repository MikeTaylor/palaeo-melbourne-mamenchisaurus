# Timeline for the Melbourne _Mamenchisaurus_


## Dates

* **1979.** Pat Vickers-Rich visited the Institute of Vertebrate Paleontology and Paleoanthropology (IVPP) in Beijing to work on a Chinese/English palaeontological dictonary with Professor Minchen Zhou (Trusler et al. 2010:65).
* **1980.** Tom Rich attended a reception for visiting scientists from China. An entomologist from the delegation showed Rich photographs of the _Mamenchisaurus hochuanensis_ holotype skeleton ane explained that it going to be exhibited in Japan. Rich was immeditely interested in the idea of exhibiting it in Australia, so introduced the entomologist to Barry Wilson, director of the National Museum of Victoria. Rich and Wilson planned to request a loan (Trusler et al. 2010:64; Rich and Vickers-Rich 2020:21-22).
* **1981.** Pat Vickers-Rich made a second visit to Beijing to continue her collaboration with Minchen, director of IVPP. She gave him a letter wirten to the museum director requesting the loan, and he helped to reach an informal agreement (Trusler et al. 2010:47, 65; Rich and Vickers-Rich 2020:22-23).
* **1982.** Professor Jim Warren of the Board of the National Museum of Victoria, flew to Beijing to negotiate a formal agreement. At Rich's suggestion, he also negotiated the right for the NMV to make moulds of the two loaned dinosaurs, _Mamenchisaurus_ and _Tsintaosaurus spinorhinus_ (Trusler et al. 65). Replicating the dinosaurs was expected to take over a year (Warren, undated).
* **25 July-8 August 1982.** Bob Jones of the Australian Museum in Sydney visits the National Museum of Victoria tp help with the moulding and casting (Australian Museum Trust, 1983),
* **Six months before the exhibition opens**. Work begins on moulding and casting the two dinosaurs, and continues during the exhibition. The work is done by a crew of Australian and visiting Chinese scientists and technicians, and extends through the period of the exhibition in Melbourne and into its subsequent appearance at the Australian Museum in Sydney (Trusler et al. 2010:66).
* **1982 but when?** _Dinosaurs from China_ exhibition opens at the National Museum of Victoria, Melbourne, under the presidency of Professor J. W. Warren. It includes two Chinese dinosaurs plus some material from Australia (Warren, undated). [1]
* **27 January 1983.** The mould of _Tsintaosaurus_ is complete but that of _Mamenchisaurus_ is only about 15% done (Anonymous 1983). One public gallery at the Melbourne museum has been closed and converted to a workshop.[2]
* **6 February 1983.** The _Dinosaurs from China_ exhibition closes. The intention is to complete the molds by 18 August, when the skeletons were to go on exhibition a the Australian Musuem, Sydney before returning to China, but it seems the work had to continue in Sydney (see above).
* **When?** The cast skeleton of _Mamenchisaurus_, having been completed, is first exhibited in a low-necked posture similar to that used in the _Dinosaurs from China_ exhibition.
* **When?** Rich notices that the anterior margin of the _Mamenchisaurus_ acetabulum is more robust than the dorsal margin and wonder whether it reared.
* **22 October 1989.** The _Dinosaurs Alive!_ exhibition opens at the Museum of Victoria, intending to continue until February 1990 (Boddington 1989)[3]. It includes the _Mamenchisaurus_ cast in a near-vertical rearing posture.
* **When?** The skull having been missing from the _Mamenchisaurus hochuanensis_ specimen, a diplodocine skull had been used in its place. With the discovery of the skull of _M. youngi_ (Pi et al. 1996), Tom Davies, a Museum
Victoria technician, created a skull sculpture which replaced this.
* **4 February 1990.** The _Dinosaurs Alive!_ exhibition closes (Anonymous 1989).
* **Subsequently.** Numerous casts of _Mamenchisaurus hochuanensis_, made from the molds created in Australia, have been mounted around thw world, including Trzic, Slovenia; Copenhagen, Denmark; and Los Angeles and Chicago, USA.
